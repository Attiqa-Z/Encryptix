Biography

Imran Khan, born on October 5, 1952, in Lahore, Pakistan, is a renowned cricketer, philanthropist, and politician. He captained the Pakistan cricket team to its first World Cup victory in 1992. Post-retirement, Khan founded the Shaukat Khanum Memorial Cancer Hospital in 1994, offering free cancer treatment to the needy. In 1996, he entered politics by establishing the Pakistan Tehreek-e-Insaf (PTI) party, advocating for justice and reform.
